MQTT Case Study

MQTT, short for Message Queuing Telemetry Transport, is a lightweight messaging protocol that's ideal for the Internet of Things (IoT). Designed for connections with remote locations where a small code footprint is essential and network bandwidth is at a premium, MQTT operates on a publish/subscribe model, making it highly scalable and efficient for real-time data distribution. Its simplicity and ease of implementation have made it a popular choice for a wide range of IoT applications, from home automation to industrial control systems.

The following are some of the main components of MQTT

  • Broker: The central server that manages message distribution, ensuring messages reach the correct subscribers.

  • Publisher: The client that sends messages to the broker, assigning them to specific topics.

  • Subscriber: The client that receives messages from the broker by subscribing to specific topics it's interested in.

  • Topic: A string that the broker uses to filter messages and distribute them to subscribers.

For this case study we're going to consider remote monitoring of heavy machinery through MQTT.

  • Broker: A free cloud based broker with url 'ws://broker.emqx.io:8083/mqtt'
  • Publisher: A big truck equipped with IoT device able to publish to our broker
  • Subscriber: A remote monitoring station simulated by the popup window (once you click start)

Once you click "Start" the big truck is ready to start publishing and the Gas Pedal button becomes active. When you hover over the button the truck will accelerate, when you drag the mouse away from the button the truck will decelerate.T he truck will be publishing its speed, fuel level, and distance traveled to the broker, and the popup window will be recieving those values from the broker. When you click "Stop" the truck stops publishing and the speed, fuel, and distance values will be reset.
